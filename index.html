About the project

During this showcase project for the minor CDT (Creative Design and technology) I tried to automate my own rollerblinds. Over the last 8 weeks I worked on this while exploring different 3d modelling software, testing 3d printer materials, gathering materials and creating a contraption that would automate my roller blinds. This page will take you through eight weeks the last 8 weeks as of the time of writing this page.

I hope reading this page will be as fun to read as I experienced the last 8 weeks.


Happy reading,
Jordy Grooten

Exploration of project

During the exploration phase, I tried out different 3d modelling software. Here I tried out OnShape, Blender and Autodesk Fusion. Here I tried to create different objects in 3d.
For instance, I modelled a donut when exploring Blender. Which you can see at the section photos here.

After the exploration of the 3d modelling software, I started exploring different kinds of materials to use to keep everything together. The materials I explored were PLA, PLA tough and ABS. For with these materials it was possible to print out with my own 3d printer.

Lastly I explored different types of motors I could use to eventually make a choice of using a stepper motor.

Development project

During the Development I put together the materials I needed and started building. Then I started working with a stepper motor. First I tried to make the motor move with just code. When that worked I implemented two buttons to make it move clockwise and counter-clockwise. In the meantime I also was designing the holder of the circuitry which should fit on the holder of the rollerblinds. Then I implemented a temperature and humidity sensor in the circuitry and added code to where the rollerblinds would roll down when a certain temperature is met. After that I implemented more user interaction by adding an infrared receiver with a remote. This way the blinds can be controlled from the other side of the room when needed.
